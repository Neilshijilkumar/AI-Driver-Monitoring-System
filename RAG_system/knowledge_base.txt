MOBILE PHONE DISTRACTION

Using a mobile phone while driving is extremely dangerous because it diverts attention from the road and slows reaction time.

Mobile phone usage includes calling, texting, browsing, or holding the phone while driving.

Even a few seconds of distraction can significantly increase accident risk.

---

DRINKING WHILE DRIVING

Drinking water or beverages while driving reduces focus and delays reaction time.

Drivers should avoid multitasking while operating a vehicle.

Taking one hand off the steering wheel increases accident risk.

---

HEAD DOWN POSTURE

Head down posture indicates possible phone usage or drowsiness and increases accident risk.

Looking down reduces awareness of road conditions and surrounding vehicles.

---

LOOKING SIDEWAYS

Looking sideways for a prolonged period reduces road awareness and increases accident probability.

Drivers may miss traffic signals and sudden obstacles.

---

DROWSY DRIVING

Driver drowsiness reduces alertness and may lead to microsleep.

Fatigue can cause lane drifting and delayed braking.

---

SAFE DRIVING

Safe driving requires full attention, both hands on the wheel, and eyes focused on the road.
